Share Your Notion Database with Your Integration

Important: For your Notion integration to access a database, you must explicitly share the database with it. This is a security measure to ensure integrations only access the data you want them to.

Why doesn't my integration see my database?

Even though you created the integration, Notion's security model requires you to explicitly share each database with it. Your integration named RS_NTN is currently not connected to any databases, which is why you're seeing the error:

Could not find database with ID: 15c3faca-41ea-4a69-aabc-44f3b599eb73

Step-by-Step Instructions

1 Open your database in Notion

Navigate to the database you want to share with your integration.

[Image: Notion database view]

2 Click the "Share" button

Look for the "Share" button in the top-right corner of your Notion page and click it.

[Image: Share button highlighted in the Notion interface]

3 Invite your integration

In the sharing modal:

  1. Type RS_NTN in the search field
  2. Select your integration when it appears
  3. Click "Invite"
[Image: Sharing modal with integration being invited]

4 Run the test again

After sharing your database with the integration, run your test script again:

node server.js

This time, your integration should be able to access and query the database!

Note: If your database has a parent page, make sure to share the database itself, not just the parent page.

Still having trouble?

Check that:

  • The database ID in your .env file matches the actual database ID
  • Your integration token is correct
  • Your integration has the necessary capabilities enabled in the Notion dashboard

For more detailed information, see the official Notion documentation.
